Source: Genesis 1-3 (summary seed)

A singular creator calls order out of formless waters through spoken decree. Light, sky, land, celestial signs, living creatures, and humanity emerge in structured sequence. Humanity receives dignity and stewardship, with breath and vocation tied to divine intent. The garden narrative introduces intimacy, moral boundary, deception, rupture, and exile, while preserving a forward-looking thread of restoration.

Comparative hooks:
- Ordered cosmos from primordial waters.
- Speech or decree as a creative force.
- Human identity as image-bearing or representative.
- Moral transgression reshaping the human-divine relationship.
