Source: Rig Veda, Hymn of Creation (summary seed)

The hymn contemplates beginnings with philosophical restraint: before distinction, categories of being and non-being blur. Desire appears as an early principle, and the text questions whether any observer can fully know origins. Rather than fixed narrative certainty, the hymn frames creation as mystery approached through reverent inquiry.

Comparative hooks:
- Pre-cosmic ambiguity prior to ordered distinctions.
- Desire or intention as a generative motif.
- Epistemic humility about ultimate origins.
- Sacred speech as contemplation rather than decree.
